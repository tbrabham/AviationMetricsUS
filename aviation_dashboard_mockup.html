📊 What is the Disruption Score?

The disruption score is a 0–100 daily severity index that combines flight delay volume, cancellation rate, and active FAA ground programs into a single number — making it easy to compare days and spot trends at a glance.

Formula
Score = (delays ÷ 5,601 baseline) × 28
+ (cancels ÷ 340 baseline) × 38
+ (ground stops) × 8
+ (ground delay programs) × 4
── capped at 100 ──────────────────

What Each Level Means for OTA Workforce
Normal (0–25) — Routine background delays. Standard staffing. No proactive action needed.
Moderate (26–50) — Elevated delays across 1–2 hubs. Expect a modest contact volume uptick. Monitor closely.
High (51–75) — Major storm or multi-hub event. Significant rebooking demand. Consider staffing up.
Critical (76–90) — System-wide disruption. High cancellation rate. Proactive customer outreach recommended.
Crisis (91–100) — Extreme event (e.g. Easter 2026 storm sequence). All-hands response. Expect wave of contacts 24–48 hrs.
Important Note

The score is a directional risk signal, not a precise operational metric. It's most useful for day-over-day comparison and early-warning staffing decisions. Daily data is sourced from the FAA NAS Status API and reflects morning conditions — end-of-day totals may differ.
